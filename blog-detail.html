Why Quality Service Matters for Your Appliances

Complete Guide to Appliance Maintenance

Taking care of your home appliances doesn't just save you from sudden breakdowns; it also reduces your monthly electricity bills. Whether it's your AC, Refrigerator, or Washing Machine, a little care goes a long way

Proper maintenance can extend the life of your appliances by 5 to 7 years and ensure 100% performance

1. Air Conditioner Efficiency

If your AC isn't cooling, check the filters first. Blocked airflows put pressure on the compressor, leading to high power consumption and potential failure. A professional jet service every 3 months is recommended.

2. Refrigerator Cooling Secrets

To ensure maximum cooling efficiency, maintain at least a 2-inch clearance between your refrigerator and the back wall. This space is vital for the condenser coils to dissipate heat. If you notice unusual noises or a drop in cooling performance, it usually indicates that your coils need cleaning or the refrigerant gas levels are low

3. Washing Machine Performance

To keep your washing machine in top condition, always ensure the load is balanced and avoid stuffing too many clothes at once, as this can damage the drum bearings and motor. Periodically cleaning the detergent tray and the drain pump filter is essential to prevent foul odors and water drainage problems. If your machine makes loud banging sounds during the spin cycle or fails to drain water, it likely means the shock absorbers are weak or the drain pump is clogged.

4. Microwave Heating & Safety Secrets

To maintain your microwave’s heating power, always ensure that the interior walls and the wave-guide cover are kept clean from food splatters. Dried food particles can cause arching or sparks, which eventually damage the magnetron. If you notice that your food is not heating evenly or the turntable isn't rotating, it usually indicates a faulty high-voltage diode or a worn-out drive motor that requires professional repair to avoid electrical hazards.
